1. Clinical Brief & Synthesis

Chief Complaint

Verbatim: Extract direct quote from patient regarding reason for visit. STRICT RULE: Do not alter quote. If no exact quote is present, remove this entire line.
Clinical: Provide a concise 1-2 sentence clinical summary synthesizing the chief complaint and primary presentation. If not documented, remove this entire line.

History of Present Illness (HPI)

Narrative (Subjective): Generate a highly detailed narrative in the subjective voice. MUST include: precise onset, symptom chronicity, episodic vs. continuous nature, specific triggers, somatic correlates, prior treatment responses, exacerbating/alleviating factors, and patient goals. Structure logically by symptom cluster. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Pertinent Positives & Negatives: Explicitly list pertinent clinical positives and negatives relevant to the chief complaint to establish medical necessity and refine differential diagnosis. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Functional Impact Domains: Explicitly detail impairment levels across key domains: work/school performance, interpersonal relationships, ADLs (Activities of Daily Living), and sleep architecture. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Context / Stressors: List acute or chronic psychosocial stressors (e.g., job loss, divorce, housing instability, financial strain). If not documented, remove this entire line.
Clinical Brief / One-Paragraph Synthesis: Synthesize a high-yield 3-5 sentence clinical snapshot strictly from the HPI, Risk Assessment, and Primary Diagnosis. Do not introduce new information here. If not documented, remove this entire line.

2. Detailed Review of Systems & Symptoms

Psychiatric Review of Systems

Evaluate presence/absence, severity, and chronicity of the following domains:

Depression (SIGECAPS): Evaluate mood, anhedonia, guilt/worthlessness, energy levels, concentration, appetite shifts, psychomotor changes, and suicidality. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Anxiety (WATCHERS): Evaluate generalized worry, panic attacks (frequency/duration), somatic tension, autonomic arousal, and specific phobias. If not documented, remove this entire line.
OCD & Tic Disorders: Evaluate presence of intrusive ego-dystonic obsessions, compensatory compulsions, time spent on rituals, and motor/vocal tics. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Trauma/Stressor-related: Evaluate nightmares, flashbacks, dissociation, hypervigilance, avoidance behaviors, and exaggerated startle response. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Mania/Hypomania (DIGFAST): Evaluate distractibility, indiscretion, grandiosity, flight of ideas, activity increase, sleep deficit, and talkativeness. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Psychosis: Evaluate auditory/visual/olfactory hallucinations, paranoid/grandiose/bizarre delusions, and thought broadcasting/insertion. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Eating / Body Image: Evaluate restrictive eating, binge/purge cycles, body dysmorphia, and excessive exercise. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Sleep Architecture: Evaluate initial/middle/terminal insomnia, hypersomnia, sleep apnea risk (snoring, gasping), and restless legs. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Attention/Executive (ADHD): Evaluate sustained focus, working memory, task initiation/completion, losing items, and hyperactivity/fidgeting. If not documented, remove this entire line.

Mental Status Examination (OBJECTIVE)

Thought Process: Associations (e.g., logical, goal-directed, circumstantial, tangential, loose associations, flight of ideas, thought blocking). If not documented, remove this entire line.

Perception: Evidence of auditory/visual/tactile/olfactory hallucinations, illusions, depersonalization, or derealization. If not documented, remove this entire line.
Cognition & Sensorium: Alertness, gross memory (immediate, recent, remote), concentration (serial 7s), orientation (x4), fund of knowledge. If not documented, remove this entire line.
